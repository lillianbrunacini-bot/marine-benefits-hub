BAH and BAS are completely tax-free
Only base pay gets taxed. BAH (housing) and BAS (food) come out untouched, which makes your real compensation much higher than it looks on paper.

You keep what you don't spend
BAH is based on median rent in your area. If you find a place cheaper than your BAH rate, the leftover is yours to keep — tax-free. It's not a use-it-or-lose-it thing.

Going overseas? It's called OHA instead
OCONUS stations use Overseas Housing Allowance (OHA) instead of BAH. It covers your actual rent up to a local cap, plus a utilities allowance. Rates vary a lot by country — ask the AI tab for your specific location.

Start documenting injuries now, not later
VA disability is based on proving a service connection. Every injury, exposure, or medical issue you document while on active duty makes a future claim stronger. Don't wait until you're separating.

PACT Act — burn pit exposure
If you were near burn pits or had toxic exposures, file a claim under the PACT Act. The burden of proof has flipped — the VA now has to prove you're NOT eligible. Always worth filing.

Acronym dictionary
BAH
Basic Allowance for Housing
Monthly tax-free payment to cover rent. Based on rank and duty station. If you rent for less than your BAH, you keep the difference.
BAS
Basic Allowance for Subsistence
Monthly tax-free food allowance. Officers get $311/mo, enlisted get $460/mo (2026). You get it regardless of whether you eat in the chow hall.
PCS
Permanent Change of Station
When your Marine gets orders to move to a new base. Usually happens every 2–3 years. The government pays for your move.
MOS
Military Occupational Specialty
Your Marine's job. Every job in the Corps has a 4-digit code. 0302 = Infantry Officer. 0311 = Rifleman. 6174 = Helicopter mechanic.
LES
Leave and Earnings Statement
Your Marine's monthly pay stub. Shows base pay, all allowances, deductions, and leave balance. Available on MyPay.dfas.mil.
DEERS
Defense Enrollment Eligibility Reporting System
The military's database of who's eligible for benefits. You must be enrolled in DEERS to use TRICARE, get an ID card, or access base. Do this first thing after marriage or birth.
NCO
Non-Commissioned Officer
Enlisted leaders — Corporal (E-4) through Sergeant Major (E-9). They're promoted from within the enlisted ranks, not commissioned from college/OCS.
OCS
Officer Candidate School
Where civilians become Marine officers. 10 weeks in Quantico, VA. If your Marine went to college and joined as an officer, they went through OCS or NROTC.
TAD / TDY
Temporary Additional Duty
Short-term assignment away from home base for training, schools, or missions. Not a full PCS — they come back. BAH stays the same.
CO
Commanding Officer
The boss of a unit — company, battalion, or regiment. What civilians would call the CEO of that unit. Has a lot of authority over your Marine's daily life.
XO
Executive Officer
Second in command of a unit. Handles the admin and day-to-day operations so the CO can focus on leadership and mission.
SNCO
Staff Non-Commissioned Officer
Staff Sergeant (E-6) and above. More experienced NCOs who serve in staff or advisory roles rather than directly leading small teams.
FRO
Family Readiness Officer
The unit's designated contact for military families. Your first call when you can't reach your Marine, need info about deployment, or need help navigating resources. Get this number early.
FRG
Family Readiness Group
The spouse and family community for a unit. Hosts events, shares info during deployments, and is a great way to meet other military spouses. Some are very active, others less so.
USMC
United States Marine Corps
One of the six branches of the U.S. Armed Forces. Known for infantry and expeditionary warfare. Administratively part of the Department of the Navy.
UA
Unauthorized Absence
The Marine equivalent of AWOL (Absent Without Leave). Missing a formation or duty without permission. A serious offense that can affect pay and career.
TLE
Temporary Lodging Expense
Reimbursement for hotel/temp housing during a PCS move. Up to 10 days covered. Claim it — most people forget.
SCRA
Servicemembers Civil Relief Act
A federal law that protects active duty military financially. Caps interest rates at 6% on pre-service debt, protects against eviction, and more. Very useful during deployment.
MTF
Military Treatment Facility
The hospital or clinic on base. TRICARE is free here. If you have a choice, always use the MTF first before going off-base.
MOL
Marine OnLine
The Marine Corps' internal admin portal. Where Marines apply for Tuition Assistance, check leave balances, and manage personal records.
