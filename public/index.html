BAH and BAS are completely tax-free
Only base pay gets taxed. BAH (housing) and BAS (food) come out untouched, which makes your real compensation much higher than it looks on paper.

Combat zone = tax-free base pay
If you're deployed to a designated combat zone, your entire base pay becomes tax-free for that month — even if you were only there for one day. BAH and BAS are always tax-free regardless.
Good news: Active duty members pay $0 for TRICARE. Your coverage is automatic and free.
Pick your plan
TRICARE Prime
Best for most
Like an HMO. One primary doctor who refers you everywhere. Free at base clinic, tiny copay off-base.
TRICARE Select
Like a PPO. See any authorized doctor without a referral. More flexibility, slightly higher cost.
Direct Care
Base clinic only. Totally free, but you're limited to what's available at your installation.
TRICARE Overseas
For OCONUS assignments. Works at host-country facilities and overseas military clinics.

You keep what you don't spend
BAH is based on median rent in your area. If you find a place cheaper than your BAH rate, the leftover is yours to keep — tax-free. It's not a use-it-or-lose-it thing.

How BAH Works
What BAH coversRent, renter's insurance, and basic utilities allowance
Rate is based onYour grade, dependent status, and duty station ZIP code
It's tax-freeBAH is never taxed — it doesn't count as income
Two tiers onlyWith dependents or without — more dependents don't increase it
Rate protectionIf BAH drops, your rate is frozen until it rises again
Living in barracksBAH is waived — housing is provided at no cost to you

Utilities on baseUsually included, but some privatized homes have usage limits

Going overseas? It's called OHA instead
OCONUS stations use Overseas Housing Allowance (OHA) instead of BAH. OHA covers your actual rent up to a local cap, plus a utilities allowance and a move-in allowance (MIHA). Rates vary significantly by country — Okinawa and mainland Japan OHA rates are generally generous. Ask the AI tab for your specific location.

Start documenting injuries now, not later
VA disability is based on proving a service connection. Every injury, exposure, or medical issue you document while on active duty makes a future claim stronger. Don't wait until you're separating.

PACT Act — burn pit exposure
If you were near burn pits or had toxic exposures, file a claim under the PACT Act. The burden of proof has flipped — the VA now has to prove you're NOT eligible. Always worth filing.
